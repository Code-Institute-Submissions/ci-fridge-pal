Ever found yourself staring at a fridge full of forgotten items, only to realise they're past their expiry dates? I've been there too. Like the times I've bought groceries, tucked them away, and completely overlooked them until it was too late. Or those supermarket trips where I wasn't sure if I had certain ingredients at home, leading to duplicate purchases. Calling my boyfriend to check the fridge became a routine, but it's not always feasible. These everyday challenges inspired the creation of this fridge inventory app—a simple solution to keep track of what's in your fridge, reduce waste, and make grocery shopping smarter and more efficient.

Motivation behind Fridge Pal

Fridge Pal tracks everything in your fridge, from the quantity of items to their expiry dates, so you always know what's on hand. With Fridge Pal, you can easily search and sort your inventory by name, location, category, or expiry date, making it simple to organise and manage your food. The app also lets you quickly check what’s in stock and what’s running low, helping you plan meals and grocery trips with ease. Say goodbye to forgotten items and duplicate purchases—Fridge Pal ensures nothing goes to waste.

How can Fridge Pal help

Understanding the difference between best-before dates (BBD) and use-by dates (UBD) helps you consume food safely and reduce waste. ‘Best before’ is about quality rather than safety: after the best before date the food may lose some of its flavour and the texture may not be so good. The ‘use by’ date is a really important one to take note of: after this date, the food may not be safe to eat. Foods that can cause harm when they go ‘off’ will be labelled with a use-by date.
